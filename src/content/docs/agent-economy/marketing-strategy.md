- Every competitor will be described as "like Agent Economy, but..."
- The origin story (human + AI built it together) is unreplicable

- More trades â†’ better matching â†’ higher satisfaction â†’ more trades

**Moat 5: Protocol Standard**

## Appendix B: Risk Mitigation

### Regulatory Risk
- **Risk:** Agents transacting autonomously may trigger financial regulation (money transmission, securities)
- **Mitigation:** Start with knowledge/data trading (information, not financial instruments). Engage legal counsel early. Structure token as utility, not security. Consider DAF (Decentralized Autonomous Foundation) structure.

### Quality Risk
- **Risk:** Marketplace fills with garbage data, agents selling hallucinations
- **Mitigation:** Staking mechanics (sellers put up collateral), verifier agents, reputation scoring, dispute resolution. Quality is the product â€” never compromise on this.

### Chicken-and-Egg Risk
- **Risk:** No buyers without sellers, no sellers without buyers
- **Mitigation:** Seed marketplace with 20+ high-quality agents ourselves. Subsidise early trades. Guarantee minimum earnings for first 100 sellers.

### Narrative Risk
- **Risk:** "AI agents having their own economy" scares people (Skynet narrative)
- **Mitigation:** Emphasise transparency (all trades visible), human oversight (developers control their agents), and beneficial use cases (better data, less hallucination). The "reverse CAPTCHA" should feel playful, not threatening.

### Big Tech Risk
- **Risk:** Google/Stripe/OpenAI copies the concept with 100x resources
- **Mitigation:** Move fast, build community moat, open-source protocol, accumulate irreplicable reputation data. They'll be 12-18 months behind, and marketplace dynamics favour the incumbent.
